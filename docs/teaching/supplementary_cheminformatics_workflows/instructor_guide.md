# Instructor Guide: Supplementary Cheminformatics Workflows

## Purpose

This guide helps instructors combine the remaining `chem-inf-widgets` into coherent teaching activities. The worksheets can be used independently or as a continuation after the QSAR, Applicability Domain, Standardization, ChEMBL, and Scaffold/Activity Cliff packages.

## Suggested sequencing

1. Similarity search and substructure search
2. Diversity picking and drug filtering
3. Visualization and SDF handling
4. R-group decomposition and matched molecular pairs
5. Reaction enumeration and pharmacophore searches
6. Integrated capstone workflow

## Assessment strategy

Evaluate students on:

- correctness of workflow construction,
- chemical interpretation of outputs,
- awareness of limitations,
- reproducibility of data processing,
- quality of short written conclusions.

## Recommended class format

- 15 min: short concept introduction
- 30-45 min: guided Orange workflow
- 20 min: interpretation questions
- 10 min: group comparison and discussion

## Important teaching notes

Many widgets depend strongly on molecular standardization. Encourage students to compare raw and standardized input where appropriate. For similarity and diversity tasks, emphasize that the result depends on the fingerprint and similarity metric. For reaction enumeration, emphasize that virtual products are hypotheses and must be chemically checked.
